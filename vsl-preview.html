VSL Preview Script - Frank Pinello

Phase 1 deliverable · Script structure, page layout, production notes for the Best Pizza Operator Course hero VSL.

Format

3-5 minute short preview VSL hosted on Wistia, embedded in the landing-page hero. Not a full 18-minute pitch - Frank's audience is craft-first, operator-first, and low on patience for long sales videos. The goal is proof-of-knowledge in under 5 minutes, then get out of the way and let the module grid close.

Optional: use existing Vice/Munchies episode clip as Day-1 placeholder while custom VSL is filmed. Swap to custom 4-min VSL on day one of the install.

Cold Open (00:00 - 00:30)

On camera, Frank, at Best Pizza kitchen or counter:

"I've been making this dough for fifteen years. Same flour, same fermentation, same oven. What changes is every operator who asks me how we do it - and then goes back to their location and realises they're missing something. Not because the process is secret. Because nobody documented it. This is the documentation."

What This Is About (00:30 - 01:30)

The problem Frank sees watching indie pizzeria owners: the craft is often there, but the systems around it - sourcing decisions, fermentation consistency, staffing for volume nights, multi-location margin management - those are learned through mistakes and nobody teaches them directly.

Frank has run Best Pizza Williamsburg through all of it - scaling from one location, managing quality across multiple spots, making sourcing decisions under real cost pressure, training staff to hit a consistent standard without Frank being in the kitchen every night.

The course is that knowledge. Not theory - the actual process, decisions, and systems Frank runs today.

What's Inside (01:30 - 02:30)

Six modules. Each one is a specific production or operations problem with Frank's documented solution:

  1. Dough and Fermentation - the flour selection, hydration, fermentation window, and proofing process Frank runs across locations
  2. Sourcing and Ingredients - the supplier relationships, cost-per-pie math, and ingredient decisions that protect margin
  3. Oven Management - calibration, rotation timing, temperature floors, the small adjustments that separate consistent from variable
  4. Staffing and Training - hire criteria, the training sequence that gets a new team member to consistent pies in two weeks, retention
  5. Multi-Location Operations - the daily check-ins, the quality-control systems, the decision points that change from location one to location two
  6. Indie Pizzeria Margin and Pricing - the cost structure, menu pricing logic, and margin floor Frank uses to keep the business healthy while keeping prices honest

Who This Is For (02:30 - 03:00)

This is for: indie pizzeria owners with one or two locations who want the documented version of how Best Pizza Williamsburg actually runs. Operators who want consistent production, not just good days. Anyone who's watched the @bestpizza33 process reels and thought "I need to learn this."

This is not for: chains, franchises, or home-pizza enthusiasts who want a hobby upgrade. The course is built for operators running a real location.

The CTA (03:00 - 03:30)

"The button is below. Six modules, one monthly live session, lifetime access. You'll install the first thing in the dough module before the end of the week. - Frank."

Production Notes

  • Filming location: Best Pizza Williamsburg kitchen (preferred) or counter with pies in shot.
  • Camera: iPhone 15 Pro or mirrorless - whatever Frank has access to. Natural kitchen light is enough for a first version.
  • Wardrobe: working gear - same as any Best Pizza shift. No overdressing.
  • Pace: conversational, direct. Frank's natural speaking register on process is the asset. No scripts read to camera - brief notes at most.
  • Music: none. Kitchen ambient sound is fine under B-roll. Silent talking head.
  • Thumbnail (Wistia poster): Frank at the oven or stretching dough. No text overlay needed - the page headline does that job.

Short Cuts (for paid traffic + IG)

From the VSL, edit: - 3 x 45-60 sec hook-led clips (cold open + one module tease each) - 6 x 20-30 sec process clips - one from each module (these feed the IG content calendar) - 1 x 90 sec "Director's Cut" for paid traffic top of funnel
