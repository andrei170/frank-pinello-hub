Foundation

Copy Strategy - Frank Pinello

Foundation doc · Voice, brand pillars, headline bank, framing AG1 will write to.

Voice

Direct, craft-obsessed, street-level NYC. Frank's brand register is the working pizza operator - not the food influencer, not the chef-celebrity. Anti-soft, pro-craft. The voice respects indie operators - never talks down to them, never uses food-media hype language. The tone treats indie pizzeria owners as peers, not students.

Use: craft, process, dough, sourcing, margin, system, operator, consistent, location, technique, the work, built, runs Avoid: passion project, artisanal (overused in food space), game-changer, curated, elevated, journey, viral, foodie, secret recipe, hustle, manifest

Brand Pillars

  1. The Craft First - everything Frank teaches starts from the actual process: flour, fermentation, temperature, timing. No shortcuts, no theory divorced from production.
  2. Operator Knowledge - this is built by someone who runs multiple locations for a living, not someone who consulted on pizza. The multi-location proof is the authority.
  3. The Best Pizza Standard - the name carries weight. Best Pizza Williamsburg is a real place people visit, review, and return to. The course is the documented version of what makes that standard real.
  4. Street-Level Authenticity - NYC origin story, Williamsburg before it got expensive, Vice cameras in the actual kitchen. Frank's credibility is rooted in a specific, observable place.

Headline Bank

For the Best Pizza Operator Course landing page, hub callouts, and ad creative:

  • Run a pizzeria that produces consistent pies, not just good days.
  • The dough process, the sourcing, the margin math. Finally documented.
  • From one location to three - the system behind Best Pizza Williamsburg.
  • What the Vice cameras saw. What they didn't. The full picture for indie operators.
  • Six modules. Built by someone who actually runs the locations.
  • Stop guessing on sourcing. Here's what we actually use.
  • The indie pizzeria operating playbook. From the guy behind Best Pizza.
  • Dough, oven, staffing, margin. In that order.

Framing for the Funnel

  • Top of funnel (checklist, ads, IG hooks): craft-specific, operator-pain led. "Your fermentation window is inconsistent, your sourcing margin is leaking, your new hire is winging the dough on a Saturday night" - real production hooks beat lifestyle hooks.
  • Mid funnel (course landing, VSL): Best Pizza Williamsburg as the bridge. The restaurant built the proof; the course packages it for operators who can't visit.
  • High funnel (community / coaching path): operator-to-operator framing, direct access to Frank, "you've got the location, here's the room where we work on the systems."

Banned Phrases

Universal AG1 ban list, plus Frank-specific: - No "artisanal" (overused in the entire food-media space) - No "passion" without a concrete operator frame - No "elevated" (food-media buzzword) - No "foodie" (Frank's audience is operators, not enthusiasts) - No "viral" (process reels work because they're real, not because they chased virality) - No first-month-lift language without the Best Pizza grade caveat

Tone Calibration By Surface

Surface Register Length
IG hook Craft-specific, one operational detail 1 line
Ad copy Operator-led, process + outcome 2-3 lines
Email subject Specific to a pizza production problem 5-8 words
VSL opening "I've been making this dough for fifteen years. Here's what I know." 1 sentence cold open
Landing hero What's at stake for an indie operator who can't get consistent 1 promise + 1 mechanism
FAQ Direct answers, no hedge 2-3 sentences each

Voice Test

Every paragraph passes if an indie pizzeria owner reading it nods at the production-specific detail and thinks "he actually runs locations." If it could appear in a generic food-media "passion for pizza" piece, rewrite it.
